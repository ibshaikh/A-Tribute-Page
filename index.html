Khabib Nurmagomedov

September 20, 1988

Early Life

Khabib Abdulmanapovich Nurmagomedov was born on September 20, 1988, in the village of Sildi in the Tsumadinsky District of Dagestan, Russia. He grew up in a family of wrestlers, with his father Abdulmanap Nurmagomedov being his primary coach and mentor.

Career Highlights

Throughout his career, Khabib Nurmagomedov achieved numerous milestones, including:

  • UFC Lightweight Champion (April 2018 - March 2021)
  • Longest undefeated streak in MMA history (29-0)
  • Submission of the Year (2013)
  • Performance of the Night bonuses

Retirement

Khabib announced his retirement from professional MMA competition on October 24, 2020, immediately after his victory over Justin Gaethje at UFC 254. His decision to retire was influenced by the loss of his father and coach, Abdulmanap Nurmagomedov, earlier that year.

Legacy

Khabib Nurmagomedov is widely regarded as one of the greatest mixed martial artists of all time. His dominant fighting style, unparalleled grappling skills, and undefeated record have solidified his legacy in the sport.

Outside the Octagon, Khabib is known for his humility, respect for opponents, and philanthropic efforts. He continues to inspire aspiring fighters around the world.
